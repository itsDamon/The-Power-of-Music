Description

"Erika" is a german song, written by Herms Niel in the 30s but published only in 1938, this
song was a march song used by the Wehrmacht, the regular army of the nazi Germany; the focus
of this song is an Erica flower which is as beautiful as the girlfriend of the soldier that
sings the song. the songs start with the soldier that says in the heath bloom an Erica flower
and it attracts many bees for its wonderful fragrance and continues saying that the dress of
the girl attracts many bees as well for the same reason. the second verse says in the
countryside lives a beautiful girl which is the most precious treasure of the soldier singing.
the last verse of the song says the soldier in his room in the front, he has an Erica flower
that remembers him of the "Magdelein" (the girl) that cries for him every day at home.
This song is a distraction from the long marches that the soldiers had to do every day and
remember the loved ones that they miss so much.
