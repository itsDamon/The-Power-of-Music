Description
Masters of War" is a protest song written by Bob Dylan in the early 1960s. The song is a criticism of the military-industrial complex, and it portrays war as an immoral and destructive force that benefits only those who profit from it. The lyrics of the song are powerful and direct, with Dylan using strong language to condemn the actions of those who make war: "Come you masters of war You that build all the guns You that build the death planes You that build the big bombs You that hide behind walls You that hide behind desks I just want you to know I can see through your masks" The song goes on to criticize those who profit from war, and those who send young men to fight and die for their own gain: "You've thrown the worst fear That can ever be hurled Fear to bring children Into the world For threatening my baby Unborn and unnamed You ain't worth the blood That runs in your veins" "Masters of War" has been covered by many artists over the years and has become a classic anti-war song. Its message continues to resonate with audiences today as conflicts continue around the world.
