Description
The song expresses opposition to the leadership of White Russian general Alexander Kolchak, who is trying to establish a separatist government in Siberia.
In the song, "Apple" is a metaphor for Kolchak's power. The first verse tells how members of the Siberian Socialist Revolutionary Party (SR) praise Kolchak's authority,
but then the song goes on to criticise Kolchak as an unjust authority that people are tired of putting up with.
The song also expresses that the social revolution is underway and the power of the Soviet regime is strong.
The song also mentions the presence of the Red Army and partisans fighting Kolchak's army. The final section celebrates the fall of Kolchak and the victory of the revolution.
Overall, the song expressed enthusiasm for the Bolshevik revolution and opposition to the white counter-revolution represented by Kolchak.
