Description
this text refer to the tragic fatcs in the north of Ireland.
Thematic References
When the song was composed in the North of Ireland tragic events were taking place:
the IRA was rampant with its terrorist acts directed against the British government and the clashes between the two fronts
became more and more heated day after day. On Sunday, March 20, 1993, many families with children were out shopping for gifts for Mother’s Day.
Just that day in Warringston, Cheshire, the IRA placed bombs in two metal dumpsters.In that terrorist action 54 people were injured and 12 died.
The song is the band’s response to those atrocities that shocked everyone: not surprisingly, the wonderful lyrics of the song talk about violence,
mothers heartbroken by the loss of their children, the silence and pain caused by those terrorist acts. The musicians with this song wanted to condemn
those acts committed by some of their compatriots, taking clear distances to launch instead a message of peace.
