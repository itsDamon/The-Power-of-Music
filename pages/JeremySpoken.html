Description
He is fed up with the harassment his comrades inflict on him every day. He is ready for an extreme gesture.
One day, after telling his teacher, "Ma'am, now I’m gonna do what I came here for,"
she kills herself by shooting herself in the mouth with a 357 Magnum at her classroom door.
Eddie Vedder reads the news in a newspaper: it is enclosed in a small article. The singer is shocked: a tragedy of the kind relegated in a few lines:
"That gesture represented the revenge of Jeremy", Vedder comments that adds "the most effective revenge is to live".
That story reminds Eddie of another student, who he had argued with several years before and who had fired a gun at school.
And so he decides to write a song that is a desperate cry against that America that provides its children with means to kill
and kill themselves instead of fortifying their will. The dramatic text of the song, in which Vedder does not limit
himself to the role of narrator but expresses his emotions.
