Description
“7 miliardi” is an Italian trap song that deals with youth, current affairs, and drug issues. In the song massimo pericolo opens up and lets his cynical side transpire, his anger, some of his more personal sides, his desires and a disillusionment with his life. The author points out the strong social pressure and lack of empathy in today's society. Massimo uses strong words and powerful imagery to describe his own psychological condition and that of society, depicting the problems and difficulties that many people face every day. This creates an atmosphere of despair and discouragement in the song, although it may not seem so upon casual listening. In some verses, he also addresses the issue of drugs, and despite his use past and present use, he opposes it by calling it a derogatory adjective ("shit"). (I take this shit seriously I sell this shit seriously) (My father doesn't like this shit) He also touches on the topic of alcohol, showing how young people find solace in it: (Alcohol makes us stronger Love has made us assholes) Massimo expresses his desire to make more money in several verses: (I want more money) (There are seven billion of us I don't give a fuck about the others I want seven billion I said seven billion) (I don't give a fuck about making deals I only care about making billions) This desire also appears in other songs, such as "Piccolo Principe" where the song starts with the verse: "I don't need anything except a million euros." Massimo Pericolo then criticizes society by saying: "You all suck here, Except for me apparently," elevating himself, although he knows it's not true, as can be seen in the last verse: "I just want a decent life."
