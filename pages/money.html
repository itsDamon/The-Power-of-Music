The morbid attachment to money on the part of contemporary society generates all kinds of wickedness.
From psychoanalytic personal problems to continuing dissatisfaction with career failure, bourgeoisie
seeks to overturn that triad of traditional values (spirit-hierarchy-aristocracy).
The golden calf is all in all preferred by far to any personal and family value.
And, though criticized, money remains the real engine of the world around us.
Rimando tematica
The economic crisis has changed the habits of a very large part of the population.
The way of life has been transformed especially in the field of saving: it is spent for the things strictly
necessary and much less for those superfluous. Pulling out your wallet has become more complicated and saving one of the main goals when you leave home.
Trying not to spend too much can become an obsession turning into a real "savings syndrome".
In the United States, psychologists have renamed this disorder "hyperopia", synonymous with the better known term hypermetropia.
But why? Because, just like in the case of visual impairment, people who are obsessed with saving only have a long-term view
but cannot live in the present because of the fear of not having enough money in the future. This forced saving, however,
can cause depression and moodiness, making the present more complicated.
