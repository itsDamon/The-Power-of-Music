Description
"All Eyez On Me" is a song by Tupac Amaru Shakur, an American rapper, activist, and actor. He is considered one of the greatest and most influential rappers of all time, despite his brief music career. The song opens the album of the same title and talks about how Pac became a rap star with significant success despite the difficulties he faced in the past, such as poverty, racism experienced at school, and lack of help from the state, which gave him the strength to find an outlet in music from that situation of discomfort. In fact, the song, and the album, covers themes such as crime, politics, love, racism, and life on the streets. In the song, Pac acknowledges his position as a leader in the world of hip hop and, referring to his power, encourages all boys who identify with his past to try to improve their lives without resorting to crime. The song's lyrics reflect his desire to be respected and admired as an artist, but also the awareness of the need to protect himself and his reputation from media attacks and enemies. The song helped make the album "All Eyez On Me" one of the biggest commercial and cultural successes in the history of hip hop. It is his fourth album, and the title of the album refers to his fame and personal situation at the time, when he had many eyes on him. "All Eyez on Me" was the first hip hop album to be released after Pac was released from prison, where he served a sentence for alleged sexual assault. The album has sold more than ten million copies in the United States and has been certified Diamond by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA), becoming one of the few albums in music history to receive such certification.
