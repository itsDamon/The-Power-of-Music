Description
This song, although not one of the rapper's most famous, has aroused many criticisms: the lyrics describe not only the hypocrisy that hides behind the apparent perfect democracy and freedom of speech of the United States, which are ironically ridiculed during the song by the author, which also happens in the music video, where Uncle Sam is first shown on a propaganda poster which reads "I'll kill you" while showing the middle finger. Subsequently, in the final part of the video, the same is depicted with his usual patriotic attire as a pig supported by wires holding a press conference while behind him, we can see the White House covered in money and the explosion of an atomic bomb. The song also describes how Eminem was regarded before and after he became famous: he explains that before he became a star, no record company wanted to sign him to a contract, and that nobody cared about the colour of his skin (something for which he has been criticized quite aggressively over the years, even by rapper Benzino, who has stated that "if Eminem hadn't been born white, he would never have sold so many records". While the song may have some racial content, Marshall Mathers does nothing but joke about the colour of his skin and his blue eyes, the fact that young people consider him as a model to follow, a "guru" to be taken as an example, but above all he highlights the fact that being white , the union (and certainly not the division) between the two ethnic groups, the white and the black, which he tries to convey through music, are always misunderstood and interpreted with negative tones even by the whites themselves (there is also a reference in the video, where, in the initial part, various posters of Eminem are shown next to the garbage, a symbol of the hatred that some have towards his way of expressing himself, especially the categories of parents and politicians).
