Description
"good kid, m.A.A.d city" is the second studio album by rapper Kendrick Lamar, released in 2012.
The album is a concept album that tells the story of Lamar's youth in the city of Compton, California, and his experiences with violence, crime, and hip-hop culture.
The title of the album refers to Lamar's identity as a "good kid" amidst the violent and criminal reality of the neighborhood in which he grew up,
and the acronym "m.A.A.d," which stands for "My Angels on Angel Dust" and refers to drug use in his community.
The album is divided into chapters and features a narrative plot that follows Lamar through a night of crime and violence,
in which he and his friends are involved in a shooting, a robbery, and an incident with the police. Along the way,
Lamar deals with themes such as faith, love, family, and peer pressure to conform to street culture.
The album's music is influenced by 1990s West Coast gangsta rap, but also features jazz, funk and soul sounds.
Lamar's work has received high praise from critics for his ability to portray the reality of life in the Compton area,
as well as his ability to create a coherent and engaging concept album.
"good kid, m.A.A.d city" was a major commercial success, debuting at No. 2 on the Billboard 200 album chart and receiving numerous Grammy Award nominations.
The album is considered one of the masterpieces of contemporary rap and helped solidify Lamar's position as one of the most important rappers of his generation.
