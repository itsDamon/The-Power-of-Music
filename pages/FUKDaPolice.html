Description
the song "fuk da police" by NWA, is written with a very direct and vulgar language, which aims to denounce the abuses by the police against the African-American minority, which existed in those years (80s-90s). The film "Straight Outta Compton" tells the story of NWA, from its inception to its disbandment. In the Detroit concert scene, this song, before the performance, was forbidden to be played by the concert security police group, but this prohibition was not respected and after a few seconds from the beginning of the song, shots are heard which intuitively they come from the police, later the group is arrested. This scene is similar to what really happened, because the bangs were there but they were cherry bombs and not shots, then the restriction would have started from the inner circle of the group, the manager of the deceased Eazy-E (one of the singers of the group) Jerry Heller agreed in pre-tour negotiations with tour promoter Darryll Brooks that the band would be fined $25,000 if they played the song, as Brooks feared the song would not be liked by conservative local authorities , so the police reacted to the concert on their own.
